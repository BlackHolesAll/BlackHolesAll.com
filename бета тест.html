The first concept and how we discovered it The idea of objects with such huge gravitational force was initially proposed by English scientist — John Michell in 1783. He was trying to find a method to determine a star's mass. Michell accepted Newton’s theory that light consists of numerous tiny particles. Then he reasoned that those particles, emerging from the surface of a star, have their speed reduced due to the gravitational pull of a celestial body. He wondered how strong this effect could be. John knew that at some point, the gravitational embrace of an object is so strong, that even the speed of light can’t escape it. (Modern scientists claim that his theory was far ahead of his time. However, there was still one mistake made by John Michell. According to Einstein’s Theory of Relativity, the speed of light is constant and no force can affect it).
